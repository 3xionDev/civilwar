Slavery and Abolition

Slavery was the central issue dividing the nation. Abolitionists in the North pushed for the complete end of slavery, using speeches, newspapers, and books to spread their message. Many in the South viewed these efforts as a threat to their way of life and economic system.

Events like the publication of Uncle Tom’s Cabin and acts of resistance by enslaved people increased awareness and opposition to slavery in the North. At the same time, laws like the Fugitive Slave Act angered many Northerners by forcing them to assist in returning escaped enslaved people. These developments deepened mistrust between the two regions.
