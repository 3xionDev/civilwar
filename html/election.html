The Election of 1860 and Secession

The election of 1860 was a turning point. Abraham Lincoln, the Republican candidate, won the presidency without support from Southern states. Many Southerners feared that his election threatened the future of slavery, even though he had promised not to interfere with it where it already existed.

In response, several Southern states chose to secede, or leave, the Union. Beginning with South Carolina, they formed the Confederate States of America. This marked the official breakup of the Union and made conflict almost unavoidable.
