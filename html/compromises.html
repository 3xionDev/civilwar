Compromises between the North and South

Throughout the first half of the 19th century, several compromises attempted to keep peace between free and slave states. Agreements like the Missouri Compromise and the Compromise of 1850 tried to maintain a balance in Congress, but they only provided temporary solutions. Each new territory added to the nation reopened the debate over slavery.

The Kansas-Nebraska Act of 1854 made tensions worse by allowing settlers to decide the issue of slavery through popular sovereignty. This led to violent conflict in “Bleeding Kansas,” where pro-slavery and anti-slavery groups fought each other. These events showed that compromise was becoming less effective and that the nation was growing more divided.
