The Beginning of the Civil War

Tensions finally turned into open conflict in April 1861 when Confederate forces fired on Fort Sumter, a U.S. military fort in South Carolina. After the attack, Lincoln called for troops to defend the Union, prompting more Southern states to join the Confederacy.

With both sides preparing for war, the United States was officially divided. What had begun as political disagreement and sectional tension had escalated into a full-scale civil war that would determine the future of the nation.
