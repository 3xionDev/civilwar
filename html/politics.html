Political Breakdown

As tensions increased, political parties began to split along sectional lines. The formation of the Republican Party in the 1850s, which opposed the spread of slavery, alarmed the South. Meanwhile, older parties like the Whigs collapsed under the pressure of the slavery issue.

The Supreme Court’s Dred Scott decision in 1857 further divided the nation by ruling that African Americans were not citizens and that Congress could not ban slavery in territories. This decision outraged many in the North and convinced Southerners that their rights needed strong protection, pushing both sides further apart.
