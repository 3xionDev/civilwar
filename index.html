Civil War Causes: Topic Index

The United States became increasingly divided in the years before the Civil War, as economic differences and disagreements over slavery created growing tension between regions. Efforts to maintain unity through compromises and political solutions gradually broke down, deepening mistrust and conflict. These unresolved issues ultimately led to the secession of Southern states and the outbreak of war, marking a turning point in the nation’s history.
